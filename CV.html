Sheri B. Pan
tech attorney and writer
Sheri is an attorney and writer on tech law, with a focus on privacy, copyright, and online speech. She recently clerked for the Honorable Robert B. Kugler of the District of New Jersey, where she oversaw half the civil docket and prepared bench memos, including in relation to the Judge's recent appointment to the FISC.

While at Harvard Law School, Sheri completed research on international originality standards for Professor William Fisher, authored portions of a comment to the USCO on TPMS, researched the historical beginnings of writs as related to the Apple-FBI dispute, and oversaw the print side of the Harvard Journal of Law and Technology. She has previously worked at organizations such as the EFF, CDT, Google, and the Berkman Klein Center.
